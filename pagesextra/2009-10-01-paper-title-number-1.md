excerpt: "This paper investigates the impact of the Federal Reserve (Fed) Chair's facial expressions during FOMC press conferences on market outcomes, examining whether these nonverbal cues are consciously used to influence markets and if experience enhances a Chair's control over these expressions to promote stability. I use facial emotional recognition software and neural network deepfake technology to study the Chair's information delivery. I find that as the Fed Chair's term lengthens, they relax more into the role and make more negative expressions, but these negative expressions impact the market less as the market gains more experience. Investors react differently and more towards negative expressions and to complex expressions, indicating that facial expressions are a rich source of information. The Fed Chair also does not consciously control his/her facial expressions, which may cause more volatility that keeps the press conference interesting and useful. Overall, there is a strong human element in the Fed Chair's delivery that may not be easily replaced by AI."
